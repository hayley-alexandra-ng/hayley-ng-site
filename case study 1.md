**Case Study: Experience AI Weeks (FY27)**

*From AI anxiety to (the beginning of) AI fluency: empowering a global creative org.*

*TL;DR:*

* **The Scope:** 700+ Designers, Researchers, and Ops Professionals  
* **My Role:** Lead Experience Program Manager & Instructional Designer  
* **Timeline:** 3 Weeks (May 2026\)  
* **Core Impact:** 83% Claude Code adoption, 76 unique AI prototypes built, program adopted as the enterprise blueprint.

### **The Friction (The Challenge)**

May 2026 dropped a big ask on the Experience Org: get designers and researchers up and running on Claude Code, fast. Leaders wanted the design and research community vibe coding deliverables to match the velocity already building on the Eng and PM side — and they wanted it *now*.

There was just one catch. This org is largely non-technical. Claude Code runs via CLI — a command line interface — meaning it lives in a terminal. For most folks here, that's not a tool. That's a scary black box.

I was given two weeks to build and deliver an AI learning experience for the entire Exp org, in coordination with cross-functional partners. After I picked my jaw up off the floor, I took a breath — and got to work.

The space was deeply ambiguous. Leaders didn't have a clear vision or answers. They were looking to my team to provide both.

**What the data told us (from the Pulse Survey in Phase 1):**

* Average proficiency sat at "Awareness": just 2.6 out of 5 (Scale: 1: Unfamiliar, 2- Aware, 3- Learning, 4- Proficient, 5- Expert)  
* 65% cited a complete lack of bandwidth/ protected time to even  *think* about learning something new  
* 42% didn't know which tool was actually relevant to their day-to-day work

I KNOW could've taken the easy path: teach the mechanics, check the box. Click the button , move on. But I saw a bigger opportunity – to start to change the way folks think and  build real fluency. Not just *how* to use Claude Code, but *why* it works the way it does. The kind of understanding that makes someone stronger in engineering conversations and more resilient in a world where roles are increasingly fluid. It’s definitely helped me (thank you UC Irvine)

I thought back to my own coding classes in college. For any of this to stick long-term, we had to start at the beginning — figuratively, with "Hello, World." That meant grounding people in terminal basics and the underlying logic before anything else.

### **The Execution (What I Did)**

**1.Phase 1: Diagnostic Baseline:** Pre-Program.  
Designed and deployed the Pre-Program Pulse Survey to 183 employees. Worked with my partner to transform raw data into a live dashboard to align leadership on exact gaps.

**2.Phase 2: Learning Sessions**

**Foundational Learn Week:** Across 2 weeks, with leadership buy in, produced (content strategy & executed)  8 live, design & research function-focused sessions, mapped out across a project creation narrative (from tool installation to hosting a prototype). Architected global watch parties and accessible livestreams to scale attendance.

**3.Phase 3Build Week & Custom Gallery**.  
Launched a follow up “heads down guided build week” for folks to get time to build, with optional submission. Ended up with 76 unique AI projects submitted. **Personally vibe-coded and maintained a custom GitHub Pages site** to act as the living gallery for these experiments.

**4.Phase 4: Incentivization & Packaging:** Post-Program.  
Designed a structured raffle incentive to drive engagement. Packaged the entire curriculum into a self-paced course distributed to the broader Enterprise.

### **The Proof (The Results)**

* **83%** — Org-Wide Claude Code Adoption Rate  
* **76** — Unique AI Prototypes Submitted  
* **\+220%** — Month-over-Month Increase in AI Tool Utilization
